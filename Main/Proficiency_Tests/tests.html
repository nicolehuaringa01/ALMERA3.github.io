ALMERA Proficiency Tests

Laboratories around the world may use different equipment, materials, and analytical procedures. Many prefer to continue using well-established methods that have proven reliable unless there is a compelling reason to change.

ALMERA provides its members with proficiency test (PT) samples, enabling them to evaluate their methods and results. These tests help assess accuracy, precision, and global comparability of measurement outcomes.

Specific procedures and methods are developed following ISO guidelines. To ensure their effectiveness, these procedures are comprehensive, clearly described, and readily accessible to both analysts and data users.

Proficiency Tests are designed to:

  • Monitor the analytical performance of network members and identify gaps or problem areas as part of external quality control.
  • Be conducted annually, exclusively for ALMERA members.
  • Provide each participating laboratory with a set of environmental samples for characterization of anthropogenic and natural alpha, beta, and gamma-emitting radionuclides.
  • Allow laboratories to choose which radionuclides to report, with evaluations based on these selections.
  • Include a unique emergency-response PT feature, requiring results on gamma-emitting radionuclides within three days.

Upcoming Proficiency Test

IAEA-TERC-2025-01

Sample Descriptions:

  • Sample 1: Water (500 mL) — Determination of anthropogenic and natural radionuclides using radioanalytical techniques.
  • Sample 2: Mineral Water (500 mL) — Determination of low-level uranium and other long-lived natural radionuclides (ICP-MS, alpha-particle spectrometry, LSC) and gross alpha/beta activity.
  • Sample 3: Soil (200 g) — Determination of natural and anthropogenic radionuclides using radioanalytical techniques.
  • Sample 4: Vegetation (100 g) — Determination of natural radionuclides using radioanalytical techniques.
  • Sample 5: Simulated contaminated surface sample, including a blank sample.
