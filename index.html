About the ALMERA Network

The worldwide network of Analytical Laboratories for the Measurement of Environmental Radioactivity (ALMERA) aims at providing reliable and timely determination of radionuclides in samples, such as air, water, soil, sediment and vegetation that are used for both routine and emergency environmental monitoring by Member States.

The International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) supports ALMERA activities that help Member States to provide reliable, comparable and fit-for-purpose results, which are an essential requirement for any decision based on analytical measurements to protect people and the environment. This includes the organization of annual Proficiency Tests (PTs) and interlaboratory comparisons (ILCs) for radionuclides in environmental and food samples, the provision of trainings through various workshops and webinars , as well as support for collaborative development and the validation of radioanalytical methods.

As of July 2025, ALMERA consists of 198 members representing 90 countries. You can find more detailed ALMERA in Data (2025) regarding the network's composition and activities. The Agency's Marine Environment Laboratories in Monaco and the Terrestrial Environmental Radiochemistry (TERC) Laboratory of the IAEA Physical and Chemical Sciences Division are additional members of the network. The IAEA Marine Environment Laboratories act as the central coordinator of the ALMERA network's activities.

The IAEA helps the ALMERA network maintain readiness through coordination activities such as:

  • Organizing Coordination Meetings
  • Developing standardized methods for sample collection and analysis
  • Conducting interlaboratory comparison exercises and proficiency tests as tools for external quality control

Benefits of Joining ALMERA

For environmental radioanalytical laboratories worldwide, participation in the ALMERA network's activities enables them to:

  • Demonstrate Technical Competence

    Prove and maintain their technical competence in accurately measuring environmental radioactivity through international proficiency tests.

  • Harmonize Analytical Methods

    Adopt and apply recommended, validated analytical methods, leading to methodological harmonization and enhanced worldwide comparability of environmental radioactivity measurement results.

  • Enhance Staff Capabilities

    Improve the analytical capabilities of their personnel through training, workshops, and shared best practices, ensuring they meet global standards.

  • Foster Global Collaboration

    Collaborate closely with similar laboratories in their region and across the globe, contributing to a stronger, more prepared international network for radiological emergencies.
